# Contributing to Awesome Metabolic Health

Thank you for your interest in contributing! This list aims to be the most reliable, evidence-based resource for metabolic health on GitHub. Quality over quantity is our core principle.

---

## What Belongs Here

**Accepted contributions:**

- Peer-reviewed research papers (PubMed, Cochrane, major journals)
- Official guidelines from recognized health authorities (WHO, AHA, ACSM, Taiwan MoHW/HPA)
- Open-source tools and datasets with genuine utility
- Validated calculators and clinical risk models
- Educational resources from academic institutions
- Open-access review articles and meta-analyses

**Not accepted:**

- Commercial products or services (supplements, meal plans, apps with affiliate links)
- Blog posts, YouTube videos, podcasts (unless from a recognized researcher with cited evidence)
- Resources without clear authorship or scientific basis
- "Detox" programs, MLM health products, or any claim that is not supported by clinical evidence
- Duplicate entries covering the same source

---

## Submission Process

1. **Fork** the repository.
2. **Add your entry** in the appropriate section, following the formatting below.
3. **Verify** the link is active and the resource is what you describe.
4. **Submit a Pull Request** with a brief description of what you're adding and why it belongs.

### Entry Format

For papers and articles:
```markdown
- [Title — Author(s)](URL) — One sentence explaining what it is and why it's relevant. *Journal*, Year.
```

For tools and datasets:
```markdown
- [Tool Name](URL) — One sentence description. Note any limitations or access requirements.
```

For supplements table entries:
```markdown
| Supplement name | Primary claim | Grade (A/B/C/D) | [Author et al., Journal Year](pubmed URL) |
```

---

## Evidence Grading

When adding or updating supplement entries, use the following evidence grades:

| Grade | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **A** | Multiple independent RCTs with consistent findings OR established systematic review |
| **B** | Single well-designed RCT OR consistent prospective observational data |
| **C** | Mechanistic evidence, animal studies, or conflicting human data |
| **D** | No credible human evidence, or contradicted by higher-quality studies |

If you're unsure of the grade, note "Grade uncertain — please review" in your PR and a maintainer will assess.

---

## Taiwan Resources Section

The 台灣專區 section has different standards:

- Official HPA/MoHW publications don't require additional peer review links, but must link to the official source.
- Taiwan-specific research should ideally use the NHIRD or Taiwan Biobank as data sources.
- Local tools should serve a genuine localization need (not just a translation).

---

## Formatting Checklist

Before submitting, confirm:

- [ ] The link works
- [ ] The description is one sentence, neutral, and accurate
- [ ] The resource is in the correct section
- [ ] The entry follows the formatting pattern of surrounding entries
- [ ] No affiliate tracking parameters in URLs (remove `?ref=`, `?utm_source=`, etc.)
- [ ] The paper or resource is from 2010 or later (or is a foundational classic with noted year)

---

## What If I Found an Error?

Open an issue with:
- The section and entry name
- What's incorrect (broken link, outdated information, wrong evidence grade)
- The correction, with a source if applicable

---

## Code of Conduct

Be respectful. Health is a sensitive topic — especially weight management. Avoid editorializing, and do not add resources that shame individuals or promote disordered eating patterns. If in doubt, err on the side of inclusion with a note, and let the maintainer decide.
