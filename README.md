## Understanding Metabolic Health

Metabolic health is broadly defined as having optimal levels of five markers — blood sugar, triglycerides, HDL cholesterol, blood pressure, and waist circumference — without requiring medication.

- [What Is Metabolic Health? — American Journal of Epidemiology](https://academic.oup.com/aje) — Research overview defining the five metabolic health markers and population prevalence.
- [Metabolic Syndrome Criteria — International Diabetes Federation](https://www.idf.org/e-library/consensus-statements/60-idfconsensus-worldwide-definitionof-the-metabolic-syndrome.html) — Globally accepted diagnostic criteria with ethnic-specific waist circumference cutoffs.
- [The Biggest Loser Study (NIH) — Persistent Metabolic Adaptation](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27136388/) — Landmark 6-year follow-up showing lasting metabolic compensation after extreme caloric restriction. Fothergill et al., *Obesity*, 2016.
- [Adaptive Thermogenesis in Humans — Rosenbaum & Leibel](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20935667/) — Mechanistic explanation of how the body reduces energy expenditure in response to caloric restriction. *Int J Obes*, 2010.

## Key Metrics & Body Composition

### BMI

Body Mass Index (BMI) is calculated as weight (kg) ÷ height² (m²). While it is a population-level screening tool, BMI has well-documented limitations for individuals with high muscle mass or different ethnic body compositions.

- [WHO BMI Classification](https://www.who.int/tools/body-mass-index-(bmi)-calculator) — Official WHO cutoffs: Underweight (<18.5), Normal (18.5–24.9), Overweight (25–29.9), Obese (≥30).
- [Asian BMI Cutoffs — WHO Expert Consultation](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15234451/) — Evidence for lower BMI thresholds in Asian populations (Overweight ≥23, Obese ≥27.5). *Lancet*, 2004.
- [BMI Limitations — JAMA](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jama) — Why BMI alone is insufficient for individual health assessment; the case for waist circumference and body fat percentage as complementary metrics.
- [Programmatic BMI Education Pages — metabolab.tw](https://metabolab.tw/calculators/bmi) — How health sites use pSEO (programmatic SEO) to create per-value BMI explanation pages (e.g., /bmi/27, /bmi/22) for long-tail search traffic.

**Taiwan-specific standard**: Overweight ≥24, Obese ≥27 (Ministry of Health and Welfare, Taiwan). See [Taiwan Resources](#台灣專區--taiwan-resources).

### TDEE & Basal Metabolic Rate

Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) = BMR × Activity Multiplier. Accurate TDEE estimation is the foundation of evidence-based caloric targets.

- [Mifflin-St Jeor Equation (1990)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/2305711/) — Currently the most validated formula for BMR estimation in general populations. *J Am Diet Assoc*.
- [Harris-Benedict Equation Revised — Roza & Shizgal](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/6741850/) — The 1984 revision of the original 1919 formula; still widely used. *Am J Clin Nutr*.
- [Katch-McArdle Formula](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov) — Lean-mass–based BMR formula; more accurate for athletes and individuals with known body fat percentage.
- [TDEE Calculator — Open Source Reference](https://github.com) — Community implementations of TDEE calculators in multiple languages.

**Activity Multipliers (standard)**:

| Activity Level | Multiplier |
|---------------|------------|
| Sedentary (desk job, no exercise) | 1.2 |
| Lightly active (1–3 days/week) | 1.375 |
| Moderately active (3–5 days/week) | 1.55 |
| Very active (6–7 days/week, hard training) | 1.725 |
| Extremely active (athlete, physical job) | 1.9 |

### Body Fat Percentage

- [ACSM Body Fat Standards](https://www.acsm.org/) — Recommended body fat ranges by sex and age from the American College of Sports Medicine.
- [DEXA vs BIA vs Skinfold — Accuracy Comparison](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov) — Systematic review comparing measurement methods. DEXA remains the gold standard; BIA varies by device and hydration status.
- [Essential vs Storage Fat — Lohman et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov) — Explanation of essential fat minimums (3% male, 10–12% female) and healthy storage fat ranges.

**Reference ranges** (ACSM):

| Category | Women | Men |
|----------|-------|-----|
| Essential fat | 10–13% | 2–5% |
| Athletes | 14–20% | 6–13% |
| Fitness | 21–24% | 14–17% |
| Acceptable | 25–31% | 18–24% |
| Obese | ≥32% | ≥25% |

### Visceral Fat

- [Visceral Adipose Tissue and Metabolic Risk — Neeland et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30563170/) — Overview of visceral fat's role in insulin resistance, cardiovascular disease, and inflammation. *JACC*, 2019.
- [Waist Circumference as a Proxy for Visceral Fat](https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/9789241501491) — WHO guidelines: risk threshold at ≥80cm (women) and ≥94cm (men) in European populations; ≥80cm (women) and ≥90cm (men) in Asian populations.
- [Diet and Exercise Interventions for Visceral Fat Reduction — Ismail et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22475574/) — Meta-analysis showing aerobic exercise is more effective than diet alone for visceral fat reduction.

## Dietary Approaches

### Caloric Deficit & Macronutrient Balance

- [Dietary Reference Intakes — Institute of Medicine](https://www.nationalacademies.org/our-work/dietary-reference-intakes) — Evidence-based macro recommendations: protein 10–35%, fat 20–35%, carbohydrates 45–65% of total calories.
- [Energy Balance and Its Components — Hall et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22826636/) — NIDDK mathematical model for predicting weight change from dietary interventions. *Am J Clin Nutr*, 2012.
- [Protein's Role in Weight Management — Westerterp-Plantenga et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19640952/) — Higher protein intake (1.2–1.6g/kg) improves satiety, preserves muscle mass during deficit, and increases thermogenesis. *Br J Nutr*, 2009.
- [Safe Rate of Weight Loss — Wadden et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov) — Recommended 0.5–1.0 kg per week for sustainable loss without triggering adaptive thermogenesis.

### Intermittent Fasting

Time-restricted eating protocols that reduce eating windows rather than specific food types.

- [16:8 Intermittent Fasting RCT — Lowe et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32986097/) — 12-week RCT showing 16:8 TRE not superior to caloric restriction alone when protein is matched. *NEJM Evid*, 2020.
- [IF Meta-Analysis — Harris et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29754952/) — Systematic review of 11 RCTs; IF produces equivalent weight loss to continuous caloric restriction. *J Acad Nutr Diet*, 2018.
- [IF and Metabolic Health — Sutton et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29754952/) — Early time-restricted feeding (eTRF) improves insulin sensitivity independent of weight loss. *Cell Metab*, 2018.
- [Contraindications for IF](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC8970877/) — Who should not attempt intermittent fasting: type 1 diabetes, pregnancy, history of eating disorders, underweight individuals.

**Protocols by evidence level**:

| Protocol | Window | Evidence Level |
|----------|--------|----------------|
| 16:8 (Leangains) | 16h fast / 8h eat | High (most studied) |
| 5:2 (Fast Diet) | 5 normal days / 2 at 500–600 kcal | Moderate |
| OMAD (One Meal a Day) | ~23h fast | Low (limited RCTs) |
| Alternate Day Fasting | Every other day at 25% kcal | Moderate |

### Mediterranean Diet

- [PREDIMED Trial — Estruch et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23432189/) — Landmark RCT of 7,447 participants; Mediterranean diet reduced major cardiovascular events by ~30%. *NEJM*, 2013.
- [Mediterranean Diet and Weight Loss — Mancini et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27340304/) — Meta-analysis of 16 RCTs showing Mediterranean diet produces greater long-term weight loss than low-fat diets. *J Transl Med*, 2016.
- [Mediterranean Diet Score Calculator](https://www.thelancet.com/) — Validated 14-item adherence questionnaire from the PREDIMED study.
- [Adapting Mediterranean Diet to Asian Food Cultures](https://www.mdpi.com/nutrients) — Research on replacing olive oil with rapeseed oil and Western proteins with tofu/fish equivalents.

### Low-GI Diet

- [Glycemic Index and Glycemic Load — Jenkins et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12081851/) — Original GI concept and its application to diabetes and weight management. *Am J Clin Nutr*.
- [International GI Database — University of Sydney](https://glycemicindex.com/) — Searchable database of GI values for 3,000+ foods, maintained by the originating research group.
- [GI vs GL in Weight Management — Livesey et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18175736/) — Glycemic load (GI × carbohydrate content) is more clinically meaningful than GI alone. *Am J Clin Nutr*, 2008.
- [Rice Varieties and GI — Atkinson et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18835944/) — GI values for different rice types; jasmine rice GI ≈ 89, basmati ≈ 57, cooled/reheated rice ≈ 35 (resistant starch formation).

### Ketogenic Diet

- [Ketogenic Diet for Weight Loss — Bueno et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23651522/) — Meta-analysis comparing very-low-carbohydrate keto diets to low-fat diets; small but significant advantage in long-term weight loss. *Br J Nutr*, 2013.
- [Keto and Athletic Performance — Burke et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31188449/) — High-fat low-carb diet impairs high-intensity performance despite fat adaptation. *J Physiol*, 2017.
- [Ketogenic Diet Safety — Dashti et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/14525681/) — 24-week safety study; significant improvements in BMI, cholesterol, blood glucose. *Exp Clin Cardiol*, 2004.
- [Who Should Avoid Ketogenic Diets](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7269727/) — Contraindications: pancreatitis, liver failure, fat metabolism disorders, pregnancy.

### 211 Plate Method (Harvard Healthy Eating Plate)

The 211 plate divides each meal into: **2 parts vegetables**, **1 part protein**, **1 part whole grains** — a simplified, sustainable portion-control method derived from Harvard's Healthy Eating Plate research.

- [Harvard Healthy Eating Plate](https://www.hsph.harvard.edu/nutritionsource/healthy-eating-plate/) — The scientific basis for this meal-composition framework.
- [Plate Method vs Carbohydrate Counting — Garnett et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23506618/) — Randomized trial showing plate method produces equivalent glycemic outcomes to carbohydrate counting with lower patient burden. *Diabetes Care*, 2013.
- [Portion Control Without Calorie Counting — Rolls et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov) — Evidence that structured portion methods reduce intake by 20–30% without explicit calorie tracking.

## Exercise Science

### Resistance Training

- [ACSM Resistance Training Guidelines](https://www.acsm.org/education-resources/trending-topics-resources/physical-activity-guidelines) — Evidence-based frequency, intensity, sets, and rep ranges for muscle hypertrophy, strength, and endurance goals.
- [Resistance Training and Weight Loss — Willis et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22002517/) — Head-to-head comparison: aerobic vs resistance vs combined training for body composition. *J Appl Physiol*, 2012.
- [Muscle Protein Synthesis — Phillips & Van Loon](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21660838/) — Protein requirements for muscle hypertrophy: 1.6–2.2 g/kg body weight; post-exercise protein timing window. *J Sports Sci*, 2011.
- [Progressive Overload Principle](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7706607/) — Why gradual load increases are necessary for continued adaptation; practical implementation for beginners.

### Cardiovascular Training

- [WHO Physical Activity Guidelines 2020](https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/9789240015128) — Adults: 150–300 min moderate or 75–150 min vigorous activity per week; muscle-strengthening on ≥2 days.
- [Aerobic Exercise and Visceral Fat — Ohkawara et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16985258/) — Dose-response relationship: 300 min/week of moderate aerobic exercise significantly reduces visceral adipose tissue. *Int J Obes*, 2007.
- [Zone 2 Training and Metabolic Health — Iñigo San-Millán](https://inigosanmillan.com/) — Low-intensity steady-state cardio (60–70% max HR) as foundation for mitochondrial and metabolic health.

### HIIT

- [HIIT vs Moderate-Intensity Continuous Training — Weston et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24773393/) — Meta-analysis: HIIT produces equivalent or superior VO2max improvements with 40% less training time. *Br J Sports Med*, 2014.
- [HIIT for Fat Loss — Boutcher](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21113312/) — Review of sprint interval training as an effective strategy for reducing abdominal and subcutaneous fat. *J Obes*, 2011.
- [EPOC After HIIT — Bahr & Sejersted](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1897373/) — Excess post-exercise oxygen consumption (afterburn) after high-intensity exercise; magnitude and duration. *Metabolism*, 1991.

## Chronic Disease Prevention

### Hypertension & DASH Diet

- [DASH Diet — Appel et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9099655/) — Landmark RCT demonstrating DASH diet reduces systolic BP by 11.4 mmHg in hypertensive patients. *NEJM*, 1997.
- [Sodium Reduction Meta-Analysis — He & MacGregor](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21511494/) — Reducing sodium by 4.4g/day reduces BP by 4.2/2.1 mmHg in hypertensive individuals. *BMJ*, 2011.
- [Potassium and Blood Pressure — Aburto et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23558164/) — Increased potassium intake (≥3.5g/day) reduces systolic BP by 3.5 mmHg. *BMJ*, 2013.
- [WHO Sodium Intake Guidelines](https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/9789241504836) — Adults: <2g sodium per day (<5g table salt). Most processed foods exceed this.

### Blood Sugar & Glycemic Control

- [Prediabetes Reversal — Knowler et al. (DPP)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11832527/) — 58% reduction in diabetes incidence with lifestyle intervention (7% weight loss + 150 min/week moderate exercise). *NEJM*, 2002.
- [Dietary Fiber and Blood Sugar — Post et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22218620/) — Viscous soluble fiber (psyllium, oat beta-glucan) reduces postprandial glucose and insulin response.
- [GLP-1 Mechanisms and Weight Loss](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov) — How GLP-1 receptor agonists (semaglutide, liraglutide) modulate appetite and blood sugar; 2023–2024 clinical evidence.

### Dyslipidemia & Cardiovascular Risk

- [Dietary Fat and Cardiovascular Disease — Siri-Tarino et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20071648/) — Meta-analysis of 21 prospective studies; saturated fat not significantly associated with CVD risk when replacing refined carbohydrates is considered. *Am J Clin Nutr*, 2010.
- [Framingham Heart Study — Risk Calculator](https://www.mdcalc.com/framingham-coronary-heart-disease-risk-score) — 10-year cardiovascular event risk based on cholesterol, BP, smoking status, age, sex.
- [Omega-3 and Triglycerides — AHA](https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/CIR.0000000000000574) — 4g/day prescription omega-3 reduces triglycerides by 20–30% in hypertriglyceridemic patients.

## 台灣專區 — Taiwan Resources

Resources specific to Taiwan health standards, official guidelines, and local research.

### Official Guidelines

- [衛生福利部國民健康署 — 健康體重管理](https://www.hpa.gov.tw/) — Taiwan MoHW official BMI standards: Underweight <18.5, Normal 18.5–23.9, Overweight 24–26.9, Obese ≥27.
- [國人膳食營養素參考攝取量 (DRIs Taiwan)](https://www.hpa.gov.tw/Pages/ashx/File.ashx?FilePath=~/File/Attach/14683/File_14383.pdf) — Taiwan's Dietary Reference Intakes; updated every 5 years by the national nutrition committee.
- [台灣成人肥胖防治實證指引](https://www.hpa.gov.tw/) — Evidence-based clinical guidelines for obesity management in Taiwan adults.
- [中老年人健康促進計畫 (≥65歲 BMI 建議)](https://www.hpa.gov.tw/) — For adults 65+, Taiwan recommends a higher BMI target of 22–26.9 due to sarcopenia risk.

### Taiwan Health Research

- [台灣生物銀行 (Taiwan Biobank)](https://www.twbiobank.org.tw/) — Prospective cohort of 200,000+ Taiwanese with genetic, metabolic, and lifestyle data.
- [National Health Insurance Research Database (NHIRD)](https://nhird.nhri.edu.tw/) — Taiwan's universal health insurance claims data; used for population-level health research.
- [成人預防保健服務](https://www.hpa.gov.tw/) — Taiwan MoHW free health checkup program (every 3 years for adults 40–64, every year for 65+); includes BMI, blood sugar, lipid panel.

### Taiwan-Specific Tools

- [燃脂研究所 BMI 計算機](https://metabolab.tw/calculators/bmi) — Interactive BMI calculator with Taiwan 衛福部 classification standards, pSEO variants for per-BMI-value educational content (/bmi/22, /bmi/25, /bmi/27...).
- [燃脂研究所 TDEE 計算機](https://metabolab.tw/calculators/tdee) — TDEE calculator using Mifflin-St Jeor formula with Taiwan-localized activity level descriptions and food reference data.
- [燃脂研究所 211 餐盤計算機](https://metabolab.tw/calculators/211-meal-plan) — 211 plate method interactive planner adapted for Taiwan food culture (bento boxes, night market, convenience store selections).

### Taiwan-Specific Health Challenges

- [Taiwan Dietary Survey — Sodium Intake](https://www.hpa.gov.tw/) — Average Taiwanese sodium intake is 4,600mg/day, more than double the WHO recommendation. Night market and bento culture are primary contributors.
- [Hand-Shaken Beverage Sugar Content Database](https://www.hpa.gov.tw/) — Sugar content analysis of Taiwan's ubiquitous 手搖飲 industry; published by HPA.
- [Betel Nut Oral Cancer Association — Taiwan Research](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov) — Taiwan has one of the world's highest rates of betel nut use and associated metabolic/oral health complications.
